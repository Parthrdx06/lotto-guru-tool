The Ultimate Guide to Super 66

Super 66 is one of Australia's most unique lottery games. Unlike "draw-style" games where you pick 6 or 7 numbers from a large pool, Super 66 is a raffle-style game where the goal is to match a specific 6-digit sequence.

Pro Tip: You can play Super 66 as an add-on to Saturday Lotto, Weekday Windfall, Oz Lotto, and Powerball, or as a standalone game.

How the Numbers are Drawn

Every Saturday night, shortly after the Saturday Lotto draw, the Super 66 numbers are determined. Six individual barrels are used, each containing 10 balls numbered 0 to 9. One ball is drawn from each barrel to create a unique 6-digit number sequence.

Winning from Both Ends

The most important rule in Super 66 is that your numbers must match in the exact order they were drawn. You can win by matching digits starting from the left (the first digits) or the right (the last digits).

For example, if the winning number is 1-2-3-4-5-6:

  • If your ticket starts with 1-2... you win Division 5!
  • If your ticket ends with ...5-6, you also win Division 5!

Odds of Winning

Because there are 1,000,000 possible combinations (from 000,000 to 999,999), the odds of winning the Division 1 Jackpot are exactly 1 in 1,000,000. The overall odds of winning any prize are much higher, at approximately 1 in 50.
