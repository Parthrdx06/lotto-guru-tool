What is Eurojackpot?

Eurojackpot is a major European lottery launched in 2012 as a more frequent alternative to EuroMillions. While EuroMillions focuses on record-shattering jackpots, Eurojackpot was designed to pay out its top prize more often. It is currently played in 19 countries, including Germany, Italy, Spain, the Netherlands, and all of Scandinavia.

Why Play Eurojackpot?

Many players prefer Eurojackpot because it offers 12 different prize tiers, ensuring that a wide variety of winning combinations result in a cash return. The overall odds of winning any prize are approximately 1 in 32. Because the game spans so many nations, the lower-tier prizes (like Tier 2 and Tier 3) are often significantly larger than those found in smaller national lotteries.
