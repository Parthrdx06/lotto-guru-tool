The Evolution of Mega Millions

Mega Millions is famous for its massive payouts, including the historic $1.602 billion jackpot won in Florida in 2023. In April 2025, the game underwent its most significant change in years. While the ticket price increased to $5, the game now includes a built-in multiplier on every ticket, meaning non-jackpot prizes are automatically increased by 2x, 3x, 4x, 5x, or even 10x.

How to Play

The current format requires players to choose five numbers from 1 to 70 and one gold "Mega Ball" from 1 to 24. With the 2025 rules, the starting jackpot has been boosted, and the odds of winning the jackpot were improved from 1 in 302 million to 1 in 290 million.

Bigger Secondary Prizes

The biggest draw of the new Mega Millions is the secondary prize pool. Matching just the five white balls used to pay $1 million; now, with the integrated multiplier, that prize is at least $2 million and can reach as high as $10 million without even hitting the jackpot.
