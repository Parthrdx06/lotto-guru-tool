How the Probability Works

The Australian Powerball uses a dual-drum system. The first drum contains 35 balls (numbered 1 to 35), from which 7 are drawn. The second drum contains 20 balls (numbered 1 to 20), from which the single Powerball is drawn. Because the Powerball is drawn from a separate pool, it is possible for the same number to appear in both the main set and as the Powerball.

Mathematicians refer to this as a "combination" problem. The number of ways to choose 7 numbers from 35 is calculated using the formula for combinations, which is then multiplied by the 20 possible Powerball outcomes. This results in the staggering 134 million to one odds for the top prize.

Smart Play: Systems and PowerHits

Many regular players utilize "System Entries" to cover more combinations. For example, a System 8 entry allows you to pick 8 numbers instead of 7, effectively playing 8 separate games at once. While this increases your entry cost, it significantly improves your odds of hitting multiple prize divisions simultaneously if your numbers are drawn.

The "PowerHit" entry is another popular choice. This entry guarantees the Powerball number, meaning you only need to match the 7 main numbers to win the jackpot. This reduces your jackpot odds to approximately 1 in 6.7 million, though the ticket price reflects this increased probability.
